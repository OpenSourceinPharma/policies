1.1 Context

This meeting was hosted in person at Roche, Basel.

DRAFT Policy

This policy is in a draft state is currently not in place till endorsed by the Open Source in Pharma board of directors.

Historical context
  • github.com/openpharma was initially created by Reinhold Koch (Roche - retired)
  • The first pan-company project was when Roche, Novartis and J&J collaborated in this org on visR
  • The org exists to share packages that do not belong to a working group specific Github org, and the authors do not want to host in either their own company, or a personal, Github org
  • Governance was transferred to the Open Source in Pharma board in December 2022

1.2 Repository inclusion policy

  • Requests for a repository must be for a project that has relevance to Pharma
  • The project must have a licence recognised as in the spirit of open source by the Open Source Initiative
  • The copyright holder of the repository must be stated (most commonly this is done within the license file)

1.3 Repository governance

  • The person requesting the repository willbe granted admin control of the repository, and they will be responsible for who has access, at what level, as well as the content of the repository including both the codebase and moderating comments and discussion on the repository in github.com
  • Their is an expectation placed on repositories hosted on github.com/openpharma to be a safe place, and to not breach the code of conduct

1.4 Code of conduct for repositories

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the [Contributor Covenant][homepage], version 2.1, available at [https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct.html][v2.1].

1.4.1 The repository is open sourced

  • If the repository changes the license it is released under, it must remain a license endorsed by the Open Source Initiative
  • The codebase does not contain propriety code or any code that infringes on another organisations copyright

1.4.2 Behaviour that is not acceptable

  • The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
  • Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
  • Public or private harassment
  • Publishing others’ private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission
  • Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

1.4.3 Enforcement Responsibilities

Repository admins are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

1.4.4 Enforcement Expectations

Repository admins are expected to respond to unacceptable behaviour, with a potential path of escalation being:

1.4.4.1 Correction

Community Impact: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

Consequence: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

1.4.4.2 Warning

Community Impact: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

Consequence: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

1.4.4.3 Temporary Ban

Community Impact: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

Consequence: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

1.4.4.4 Permanent Ban

Community Impact: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

Consequence: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

1.5 Repository re-hosting policy

The admins of repositories hosted on github.com/openpharma can either request to be transferred to a new Github organisation, or implement the change themselves, at any time.

The Open Source in Pharma board reserves the right to remove repositories that fail to be in the spirit of open source for Pharma or are seen to not be responding to breaches in the code of conduct for repositories.

Removing a repository requires endorsement by the board, and will follow the following process.

  1. Admins are contacted, and given 6 weeks to nominate a new github organisation to be transferred to
  2. If no new organisation is nominated, the repository will be deleted
